# ADHD Screening Questions

## Source
Based on Adult ADHD Self-Report Scale (ASRS v1.1) and DSM-5 criteria.

## Question Bank (20 items for MVP)

Scale: 1 = Never/Rarely, 2 = Sometimes, 3 = Often, 4 = Very Often

### Inattention Items

1. How often do you have trouble wrapping up the final details of a project, once the challenging parts have been done?
2. How often do you have difficulty getting things in order when you have to do a task that requires organization?
3. How often do you have problems remembering appointments or obligations?
4. When you have a task that requires a lot of thought, how often do you avoid or delay getting started?
5. How often do you fidget or squirm with your hands or feet when you have to sit down for a long time?
6. How often do you feel overly active and compelled to do things, like you were driven by a motor?
7. How often do you make careless mistakes when you have to work on a boring or difficult project?
8. How often do you have trouble concentrating on what people say to you, even when they are speaking directly to you?
9. How often do you have difficulty sustaining attention to tasks or recreational activities?
10. How often do you lose important things?

### Hyperactivity/Impulsivity Items

11. How often do you feel restless or fidgety?
12. How often do you have trouble waiting your turn?
13. How often do you interrupt others when they are speaking?
14. How often do you do things without thinking about the consequences?
15. How often do you have trouble organizing your time and meeting deadlines?
16. How often do you leave your seat in situations when you are expected to remain seated?
17. How often do you speak without thinking, saying things you later regret?
18. How often do you have racing thoughts?
19. How often do you struggle to filter distractions?
20. How often do you misplace your keys, wallet, or phone?

## Scoring

**Inattention Score:** Sum of items 1-10 (10-40 scale)  
**Hyperactivity/Impulsivity Score:** Sum of items 11-20 (10-40 scale)  
**Total Score:** Sum of all items (20-80 scale)  

**Risk Levels (based on total):**
- 20-35: Low risk
- 36-60: Moderate risk
- 61-80: High risk

**Interpretation Notes:**
- Higher scores = more ADHD-like traits
- Symptoms must be lifelong or long-standing (this screening assumes adults reporting from childhood)
- Disclaimer: This is a screening tool, not a diagnosis
